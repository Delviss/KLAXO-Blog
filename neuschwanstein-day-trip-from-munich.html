Neuschwanstein Castle Day Trip from Munich by Car (2026 Guide)

personDaniel (Klaxo Team)
calendar_todayJuly 23, 2026
speed11 Min Read

A day trip from Munich to Neuschwanstein by car is the single most rewarding excursion in Bavaria — and the easiest to get wrong on public transport. The fairytale castle that inspired Disney sits above the village of Hohenschwangau near Füssen, roughly 130 km south of the city. With a rental car from Klaxo GmbH (www.klaxo.eu) you skip the two train changes, arrive directly at the castle car park, and still have time to add Linderhof or Oberammergau. Here is exactly how to do it.

How Far Is Neuschwanstein from Munich?

Neuschwanstein lies about 130 km south-west of Munich, a drive of roughly two hours via the A7 and B17. The final approach climbs gently into the foothills of the Bavarian Alps, past Hohenschwangau Castle and the Alpsee lake. Leaving Munich by 8am beats both the tour-bus crowds and the afternoon ticket rush.

The most direct route is A96 towards Lindau, then A7 south and the B17 “Romantic Road” finale into Füssen. In winter the passes stay clear, but an AWD SUV with winter tyres makes the higher stretches effortless.

Parking, Tickets and the Marienbrücke View

Cars park in the numbered lots (P1–P4) in Hohenschwangau village; from there it is a 30–40 minute walk uphill, a shuttle bus, or a horse-drawn carriage to the castle gate. Buy timed-entry tickets online in advance — they sell out days ahead in summer and the ticket office queue can swallow an hour.

For the postcard shot, walk ten minutes beyond the castle to the Marienbrücke, the iron bridge spanning the Pöllat gorge. It is the view on every fridge magnet in Bavaria, and it is worth every step.

Combine It: Linderhof & Oberammergau

Because you have your own car, you can turn one castle into a royal double bill. Linderhof Palace — Ludwig II's intimate rococo retreat with its Venus Grotto — is about 45 minutes east and rarely crowded. En route lies Oberammergau, famous for its painted Lüftlmalerei houses and passion play. This loop is impossible to do by train in a single day but easy by car.

Frequently Asked Questions

helpHow long is the drive from Munich to Neuschwanstein?

By car it is about two hours each way (roughly 130 km) via the A96/A7 and B17. Leaving Munich early avoids the worst of the coach traffic and secures easier parking in Hohenschwangau.

helpDo I need to book Neuschwanstein tickets in advance?

Yes. Neuschwanstein uses strict timed-entry tickets that regularly sell out in summer. Book online ahead of time and plan to arrive in the village at least 45 minutes before your slot to allow for the uphill walk or shuttle.

helpCan I visit Neuschwanstein and Linderhof in one day?

Yes — with your own car it is a comfortable royal double bill. Linderhof is about 45 minutes from Neuschwanstein, with Oberammergau on the way, so a self-drive or chauffeured Klaxo day easily covers both.

helpIs a rental car better than the train for Neuschwanstein?

For most travellers, yes. The train requires a change at Füssen plus a local bus, while a Klaxo rental takes you door-to-castle-park, lets you add Linderhof, and returns on your schedule.
