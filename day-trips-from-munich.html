Day Trips from Munich: 6 Best Destinations by Car

calendar_todayJuly 20, 2026
speed12 Min Read

Munich is the perfect base for exploring Bavaria and the Alps. Within a two-hour drive you can stand under a fairytale castle, wander a lakeside village in Austria, or ride to the top of Germany's highest peak. This is the complete guide to the best day trips from Munich, with real drive times, what to see and the ideal Klaxo car for each one. If you would rather not drive, our chauffeur and transfer options take care of the whole day.

1. Neuschwanstein Castle Day Trip

The most famous day trip of all. King Ludwig II's fairytale castle rises out of the forest near Füssen, about 1 hour 45 minutes southwest of Munich. Book your timed entry ticket in advance, then walk up to Marienbrücke for the classic postcard view. Pair it with nearby Hohenschwangau Castle or the pastel town of Füssen to fill the day. The efficient Toyota Corolla Hybrid is ideal for two, while the Mercedes GLE suits families who want more space.

2. Munich to Salzburg Day Trip

Just across the Austrian border, Salzburg is only 1 hour 30 minutes away and packs a baroque old town, Mozart's birthplace and the Hohensalzburg Fortress into an easy day. Because it is in Austria, you need a motorway vignette, which every Klaxo car crossing the border already carries. The Mercedes S-Class turns the drive itself into part of the experience. For a hands-free day, our private transfer service handles the route door to door.

3. Munich to Hallstatt Private Tour

Hallstatt is the lakeside village that launched a thousand photographs. It sits about 2 hours 15 minutes from Munich in Austria's Salzkammergut. The drive along the lakes is half the reward. Because parking in the village is limited and controlled, a private tour or chauffeur makes the day far smoother. The roomy Mercedes V-Class is our pick for families and small groups who want comfort on the longer run. See the full V-Class group travel guide.

4. Rothenburg ob der Tauber on the Romantic Road

The best-preserved medieval town in Germany, Rothenburg ob der Tauber is the highlight of the Romantic Road, about 3 hours northwest of Munich. Walk the intact town walls, see the Plönlein corner and visit the year-round Christmas village. It is a longer drive, so a comfortable multi-seater like the Mercedes Vito or V-Class keeps the group relaxed both ways.

5. Berchtesgaden and the Eagle's Nest

In Bavaria's far southeast corner, roughly 2 hours from Munich, Berchtesgaden offers the emerald Königssee lake and the Eagle's Nest (Kehlsteinhaus) perched high above the valley. The mountain roads reward a capable car, so the all-wheel-drive Mercedes GLE or VW Tiguan is the sensible choice, especially outside summer.

6. Zugspitze and Garmisch-Partenkirchen

Germany's highest peak is closer than most people think, about 1 hour 20 minutes to Garmisch-Partenkirchen. From there a cog railway and cable car carry you to the summit at 2,962 metres for a panorama across four countries. In winter the same base is a world-class ski town. A VW Tiguan or Mercedes GLE handles the alpine approach with ease.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best day trip from Munich?

Neuschwanstein Castle is the most popular, about 1 hour 45 minutes by car. Salzburg and the Zugspitze are close seconds for first-time visitors.

Can you visit Neuschwanstein from Munich in a day?

Yes. The drive is around 1 hour 45 minutes each way. Book your castle timeslot in advance and leave Munich early to make the most of the day.

Do I need a car for day trips from Munich?

A car gives you the most flexibility and reaches places like Hallstatt and Berchtesgaden that are slow by public transport. For groups, a Klaxo chauffeur or V-Class removes the driving and parking entirely.

Do I need extra documents to drive to Salzburg or Hallstatt?

Both are in Austria, so you need an Austrian motorway vignette for the autobahn. Every Klaxo car crossing the border is prepared for it, and we handle the details for you.
