Bavaria in 3 Days — The Perfect Self-Drive Road Trip from Munich

personDaniel (Klaxo Team)
calendar_todayJuly 23, 2026
speed14 Min Read

If you have three days and one rental car, you can see the very best of Bavaria — fairytale castles, turquoise alpine lakes and Germany's highest peaks — in a single scenic loop. This is our flagship Bavaria road trip from Munich itinerary: a day-by-day 3-day self-drive along the spirit of the Deutsche Alpenstraße, tying together the destinations we cover in detail across this blog. Grab a comfortable car (and a one-way option if you like) from Klaxo GmbH (www.klaxo.eu) and set off.

Overview: The 3-Day Bavaria Loop

This itinerary is a hub — each day links to a full standalone guide if you want more depth. The loop runs roughly 500 km: south-west to the castles, along the alpine edge to the peaks and lakes, then east to the Königssee before returning to Munich. It works as a round trip, or with a one-way rental if you are flying out from Salzburg or elsewhere.

Day 1: Castles — Neuschwanstein & Linderhof

Leave Munich early and drive ~2 hours south-west to Neuschwanstein, the fairytale castle that opens every Bavaria trip. Tour the interiors, walk to the Marienbrücke, then continue to Linderhof Palace and the painted village of Oberammergau. Overnight near Füssen or Garmisch. Full detail: Neuschwanstein day trip guide.

If medieval towns tempt you more than castles on day one, swap in a stretch of the Romantic Road instead.

Day 2: Peaks & Lakes — Zugspitze, Eibsee & the Bavarian Lakes

From your alpine base, ride to the summit of the Zugspitze, Germany's highest peak, and walk the turquoise Eibsee below. In the afternoon, drift north into lake country — the intense turquoise of the Walchensee and Kochelsee, or the polish of the Tegernsee. Overnight lakeside or push east. Full detail: Zugspitze & Eibsee guide and 7 best Bavarian lakes.

Day 3: Emerald Finale — Königssee, then Home

Drive east into the Berchtesgaden National Park for the emerald Königssee, Germany's cleanest lake, and the silent electric-boat ride to St. Bartholomä. In summer, add the Eagle's Nest for a staggering finale before the ~2h15 return to Munich (or a one-way drop in Salzburg, just 30 minutes on). Full detail: Königssee & Berchtesgaden guide.

Which Car for a 3-Day Bavaria Road Trip?

For two people, the efficient Toyota Corolla Hybrid keeps fuel costs low over 500 km. For families, comfort or luggage, the Mercedes GLE (AWD for the alpine sections) or the seven-seat V-Class is ideal. Landing at MUC first? Combine this with our Munich airport to the Alps guide and pick the car up on arrival.

Frequently Asked Questions

helpCan you see the best of Bavaria in 3 days by car?

Yes — a 3-day self-drive loop from Munich comfortably covers the castles (Neuschwanstein and Linderhof), the Zugspitze and Bavarian lakes, and the Königssee, roughly 500 km in total.

helpWhat is the best 3-day Bavaria road trip itinerary from Munich?

Day 1: Neuschwanstein and Linderhof castles. Day 2: the Zugspitze, Eibsee and the turquoise Bavarian lakes. Day 3: the emerald Königssee and Berchtesgaden before returning to Munich.

helpDo I need a special car for a Bavaria road trip?

A hybrid like the Toyota Corolla suits couples over 500 km, while an AWD Mercedes GLE or seven-seat V-Class is better for families, luggage and the alpine sections — add winter tyres in the colder months.
