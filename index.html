Top 5 Hidden Coastal Drives in Bavaria

calendar_today October 12, 2024
speed 12 Min Read

While Bavaria is renowned for its soaring alpine peaks and historic beer halls, few travelers realize the region holds some of the most stunning "coastal" drives in Central Europe. Winding along the shores of massive glacial lakes like Chiemsee and Starnberg, these routes offer the momentum of a seaside getaway with the crisp air of the Alps.

1. The Chiemsee Circumference

Known as the "Bavarian Sea," Chiemsee offers a drive that rivals the Amalfi coast for pure visual drama. Start your journey in Prien and head clockwise. The road stays remarkably close to the water, offering frequent turnouts where you can witness the Herreninsel palace reflecting on the mirror-like surface.

2. The Sylvenstein Dam Run

For those who crave engineering marvels paired with natural beauty, the B13 road crossing the Sylvenstein Dam is non-negotiable. The turquoise water against the dark forest creates a striking contrast that is best experienced in a convertible with the top down.

Pro Rental Tips

  • check_circle Choose a vehicle with a panoramic sunroof to fully take in the towering mountain passes.
  • check_circle Book a premium SUV for the Sylvenstein run to handle the higher elevation gear shifts with ease.
  • check_circle Ensure your rental includes local toll tags (Vignette) if you plan on crossing the nearby Austrian border.

Driving through these routes isn't just about the destination; it's about the Guided Momentum of the journey itself. Each curve reveals a new perspective of Bavaria's diverse geography, from marshy lowlands to sheer limestone cliffs.
