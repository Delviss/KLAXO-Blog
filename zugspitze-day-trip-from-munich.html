Zugspitze & Eibsee Day Trip from Munich by Car

personDaniel (Klaxo Team)
calendar_todayJuly 23, 2026
speed10 Min Read

Germany's highest mountain is closer than you think. A Zugspitze day trip from Munich starts with an easy one-hour drive to Garmisch-Partenkirchen, then climbs by cable car or cog railway to a 2,962 m summit with a four-country panorama. Add the almost unreal turquoise Eibsee and the roaring Partnachklamm gorge and you have one of Bavaria's best days out. An alpine SUV from Klaxo GmbH (www.klaxo.eu) — winter tyres fitted — makes the run simple in any season.

Driving from Munich to Garmisch-Partenkirchen

The drive south on the A95 and B2 takes about an hour, one of the most scenic short hops from Munich as the Wetterstein range rises ahead. Garmisch is the launch point for everything on this trip — park at the Zugspitze valley station (Eibsee) or in town near the cog-railway terminal.

In winter the road is well cleared, but the mountain weather turns fast. A Klaxo AWD SUV with winter tyres gives confidence on the higher stretches and carries ski or hiking kit with ease.

Reaching the Summit: Cable Car or Cog Railway

There are two classic ways up. The Eibsee-Seilbahn cable car whisks you from the lakeshore to the summit in about ten spectacular minutes — a record-holding single-span ride. The Zugspitze cog railway is slower and more nostalgic, tunnelling up through the mountain to the Zugspitzplatt glacier plateau. Many visitors go up one way and down the other.

At the top, the summit terrace serves a panorama across Germany, Austria, Italy and Switzerland on a clear day, plus Germany's highest beer garden.

Eibsee & Partnachklamm: The Perfect Add-Ons

Back at the valley, the Eibsee loop is a mostly flat 7 km / 2-hour walk around water so clear and turquoise it looks tropical — the most photographed lake in the Alps. If you have energy left, drive a few minutes to the Partnachklamm, a dramatic gorge where a walkway clings to cliffs above a thundering river (spectacular frozen in winter too).

Frequently Asked Questions

helpHow long is the drive from Munich to the Zugspitze?

It is about a one-hour, 90 km drive from Munich to Garmisch-Partenkirchen on the A95/B2, then a cable car or cog railway to the summit itself.

helpCan you visit the Zugspitze in winter?

Yes — it is a year-round destination and a ski area in winter. The mountain roads are cleared, but an AWD SUV with winter tyres, standard on Klaxo alpine cars, is strongly recommended.

helpIs the Eibsee worth visiting?

Absolutely. The Eibsee is a turquoise alpine lake at the foot of the Zugspitze with a flat 7 km loop trail of about two hours — one of the most beautiful and photographed lakes in the Alps.

helpDo I need winter tyres to drive to Garmisch?

In wintry conditions, yes — German law requires appropriate winter tyres, and alpine roads near Garmisch can be snowy from November to Easter. Klaxo fits winter tyres on its alpine SUVs as standard.
